About

I am Wilfried‑Guth Professor of Constitutional Political Economy and Competition Policy at the University of Freiburg (since 2012). I earned a Diplom in Economics (quantitative track) from Kiel and a PhD from LMU Munich (2004) on public pensions and immigration. Further study visits included the University of Wisconsin–Eau Claire, Boston University, and the University of Göttingen. Before joining Freiburg, I held a junior professorship in International Economic Policy at the University of Paderborn and, between 2009 and 2011, I served as acting professor of public finance at Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz and as acting professor of institutional economics at Philipps University Marburg.

Research. My work focuses on institutions, factor mobility (especially migration), and conflict, as well as a modern interpretation of ordoliberalism in the Freiburg tradition. My papers appear in journals across economics and political science (e.g., The Economic Journal, Journal of International Economics, Journal of Public Economics, Journal of Conflict Resolution, Journal of Peace Research). I have been involved in a range of externally funded research projects. I received the University of Freiburg’s Bertha‑Ottenstein Prize (2018) for research on polygyny and conflict, and I was a Global Shifts Visiting Scholar at Perry World House (University of Pennsylvania).

Teaching & service. I teach institutional and applied micro fields, including New Institutional Economics and Law & Economics. I received the University Teaching Prize (2017) and several student awards. Within the University of Freiburg, I served as Vice Dean of the Faculty of Economics and Behavioral Sciences and chaired joint study committees with the University of Education Freiburg. I am a member of the University’s Ethics Committee and serve, among others, on the Board of Trustees of the Walter Eucken Institute and the Scientific Advisory Board of the Forum Ordnungspolitik.
